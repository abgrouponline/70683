PROJECT OBJECTIVES ANALYSIS:

1. CONTENT DEVELOPMENT:
   Public Health Wales seeks guidance materials for the Daily Active 8 Domains 
   that schools can use to drive meaningful improvement in student physical 
   activity and wellness. We understand this requires:
   
   - Evidence-based content grounded in health promotion research
   - Clear, actionable guidance for each of the 8 domains
   - Materials that respect the time constraints of busy school staff
   - Content that can be adapted to different school contexts

2. ACADEMIC RIGOR:
   The content must be supported by robust research and best practices in:
   - Childhood physical activity promotion
   - School-based health interventions
   - Behavioral change frameworks
   - Educational pedagogy
   
3. USER EXPERIENCE:
   The platform must be:
   - Engaging and visually appealing
   - Intuitive to navigate without extensive training
   - Modular to allow schools to focus on priority areas
   - Accessible across devices and user abilities

4. DIGITAL PLATFORM HOSTING:
   Content must be optimized for digital delivery with:
   - Clear information architecture
   - Responsive design principles
   - Easy content updates and maintenance
   - Scalability for future enhancements

================================================================================
                         TECHNICAL APPROACH
================================================================================

CONTENT DEVELOPMENT METHODOLOGY:

1. EVIDENCE-BASED FRAMEWORK:
   All content will be grounded in established research and frameworks:
   
   - Socio-Ecological Model for physical activity promotion
   - Behavior Change Wheel for intervention design
   - Implementation Science frameworks for sustainable adoption
   - International Physical Activity Guidelines
   
   Our content will reference and synthesize research from:
   - International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity
   - American Journal of Preventive Medicine
   - Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports
   - Global health promotion databases

2. MODULAR CONTENT STRUCTURE:
   Each domain module will be self-contained yet interconnected:
   
   - Standalone modules allowing schools to prioritize specific domains
   - Cross-referencing between related domains
   - Flexible pathways based on school maturity and capacity
   - Tiered guidance (starter, intermediate, advanced actions)

3. ENGAGEMENT STRATEGIES:
   To ensure the resource is engaging and intuitive:
   
   - Visual learning through infographics and diagrams
   - Interactive self-assessment tools
   - Real-world scenario-based examples
   - Progress tracking and achievement recognition
   - Downloadable templates and tools
   - Video content integration (if applicable)
   - Quick wins highlighted for early momentum

4. ACCESSIBILITY & INCLUSIVITY:
   - Plain language approach avoiding unnecessary jargon
   - Readability at appropriate level for busy school staff
   - Visual alternatives for text-heavy content
   - Compatibility with screen readers
   - Keyboard navigation support
   - Sufficient color contrast ratios
   - Scalable text and responsive layouts


DIGITAL PLATFORM OPTIMIZATION:

Content Format:
- HTML5-compliant markup for web delivery
- Structured content using semantic markup
- Optimized images and graphics (WebP format where supported)
- PDF downloads for printable resources
- Editable templates in common formats

Information Architecture:
- Maximum 3 levels of navigation depth
- Clear breadcrumb navigation
- Search functionality with relevant keywords
- Content tagging system for cross-referencing
- Mobile-first responsive design

User Journey Optimization:
- Homepage with clear entry points to each domain
- Self-assessment gateway to personalized recommendations
- Action planning tools integrated throughout
- Progress saving functionality (if platform supports)
- Quick reference guides and cheat sheets
